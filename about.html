Frame and Structure


The frame provides mechanical support for the physical components of the pod. It receives force from the SpaceX pusher, the air bearings, and the magnetic brakes and distributes these forces to the other components. The skin maintains laminar flow across the pod and protects the pod from outside elements. The interconnecting supports allow a modular connection between the relatively thin components of the skins and the thicker frame. Additionally, because this subsystem is primarily responsible for the volumetric space of the pod, the frame and structure team will also handle the placement of physical components within the pod.

Suspension


The suspension subsystem is responsible for creating a low-friction levitation system that the pod travels on while moving at speeds exceeding two-hundred miles per hour, and to also provide braking for the pod once it has reached its destination, along with lateral stability controls. To levitate the pod, a halbach arrays composed of Neodynium magnets will be aligned on the bottom of the pod, which will provide a repulsive magnetic force against the aluminum sub track at speed. Magnetic braking has also been determined to be ideal for our system, as Halbach arrays are able to provide sufficient deceleration in an entirely passive and reliable manner, even in evacuated conditions. Finally, in order to support the pod, the team will be installing retractable wheels that will deploy to support the pod in low-speed transit. A general overview of each system will be given, followed by a discussion of experiments conducted and subsequent empirical calculations, concluding with a discussion of the benefits of such a suspension and braking system.

Thermal Fluids

As the compressor-less pod travels past 350 mph, air flow around the body of the pod becomes choked, thus building up in front of the pod, and increasing pressure drag.
